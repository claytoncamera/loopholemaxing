HERACLITUS OF EPHESUS — SELECTED FRAGMENTS
Translated by John Burnet, "Early Greek Philosophy" (2nd ed., 1908).
Source: Project Gutenberg eBook #67097 — public domain.

Fragment numeration follows Diels–Kranz (DK 22 B).

§ THE LOGOS

"Of this Word's being forever do men prove to be uncomprehending,
both before they hear and once they have heard it. For although
all things happen according to this Word, they are like the
unexperienced experiencing words and deeds such as I explain when
I distinguish each thing according to its nature and declare how
it is."
— DK 22 B1

"Listening not to me but to the Word, it is wise to agree that all
things are one."
— DK 22 B50

§ THE RIVER

"Those who step into the same rivers ever and always chance on
different waters."
— DK 22 B12

"We step and do not step into the same rivers; we are and we are
not."
— DK 22 B49a (paraphrase)

§ FIRE AND CHANGE

"This world-order, the same for all, no god nor man has made;
but it ever was, and is, and shall be: an ever-living fire,
kindling in measures and going out in measures."
— DK 22 B30

"All things are an exchange for fire, and fire for all things,
even as wares for gold and gold for wares."
— DK 22 B90

§ KNOWLEDGE AND CHARACTER

"Nature loves to hide."
— DK 22 B123

"A man's character is his fate."
— DK 22 B119

"Much learning does not teach understanding."
— DK 22 B40

§ THE COMMON

"Those who speak with understanding must hold fast to the common,
as a city does to its law, and even more strongly. For all human
laws are nourished by the one divine law."
— DK 22 B114

§ GOD

"God is day night, winter summer, war peace, satiety hunger; he
takes various shapes, just as fire, when it is mingled with spices,
is named according to the savour of each."
— DK 22 B67
