§ THE PROEM (DK 28 B1)

"The mares that carry me as far as my heart ever longs sped on,
since they bore me over the renowned road of the goddess, which
brings the man who knows over all cities. There it was I rode;
for thereon the speeding mares were carrying me, stretching their
strength along the broad highway; for the goddesses sent them on
towards the light, when they had left behind the halls of Night.

There is the gate that bounds the paths of Night and Day. The
goddess greeted me kindly and took my right hand in hers and spake
to me these words:

'Welcome, O youth, that comest to my abode borne by immortal
charioteers and by the mares that bear thee. It is no ill chance,
but justice and right that has sent thee forth to travel on this
way, far indeed it lies from the beaten track of men. Thou must
learn all things, both the unshaken heart of well-rounded truth,
and the opinions of mortals in which there is no true belief at
all.'"

§ THE WAY OF TRUTH (DK 28 B2)

"Come now, I will tell thee — and do thou hearken and carry my
word away — the only ways of inquiry that can be thought of: the
one way, that it is, and that it is impossible for it not to be,
is the way of conviction, for it follows truth; the other, that
it is not, and that it must needs not be — that, I tell thee, is a
path altogether unthinkable."

§ THE SIGNS OF BEING (DK 28 B8, selections)

"It is uncreated and indestructible; for it is whole and without
end, alone and immovable and without end. Nor was it ever, nor
will it be; for now it is, all at once, a continuous one. For
what creation of it wilt thou look for? How and whence could it
have sprung? I shall not let thee say or think that it came from
what is not; for it can neither be thought nor uttered that what
is not is."

"Wherefore Justice does not loose her fetters and let it come into
being or pass away, but holds it fast."

"It is the same thing that can be thought and that can be."
— DK 28 B3
